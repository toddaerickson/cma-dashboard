How the credit signal works

The signal was discovered by genetic-expression programming (GEP) — symbolic regression over hundreds of candidate formulas, pooled across HYG/LQD/MUB 2008-2024. Top-performing expressions all converged to the same shape, which is what's shown below.

What each piece means:

  • vol_6m(HYG) — 6-month realized volatility of HYG monthly returns. Economic logic: spread mean-reversion. When credit has been volatile, spreads have already widened; forward returns are elevated as spreads compress. Fama-French's DEF factor. Higher vol ⇒ higher forecast.
  • sqrt(max(T10Y2Y, 0)) — square root of the 10y−2y term spread (clipped to zero; we don't go short credit on an inversion). Economic logic: early-cycle bond rally. Steepening curve ⇒ Fed cuts expected ⇒ falling yields ⇒ bond price gains and improving credit conditions. Sqrt dampens extremes. Steeper curve ⇒ higher forecast.
  • Sum (the root '+') — the two effects are additive in the evolved tree: vol-regime and curve-regime both independently push forward credit returns higher.
